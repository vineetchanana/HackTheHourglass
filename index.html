FAQs

Who can participate in Hack the Hourglass?
If you have knack of coding and pursuing any Undergraduate, Postgraduate or diploma degree, you are more than welcome to participate in the hackathon.
How much does it cost?
Zero. Zip. Zilch. Nada. Nothing. Absolutely free. Participation in Hack the Hourglas is completely free.
What are the dates?
18th and 19th July, 2020
How many members are allowed to take part as a team in Hack the Hourglass?
While the team size can range from 2 to 4 members and individual participation aka Lone Wolves are welcome too.
What do I get by attending Hack the Hourglass?
You should participate to showcase your skills and ability to solve real world problems. One of the biggest benefits of attending a hackathon is learning new skills and attaining new knowledge. Above all its for enjoying the entire journey of development. Adding to it everyone will get Participation Certificate and a chance at winning prizes worth ₹80,000+
How will project submissions work?
To participate, click on "Apply with Devfolio" button. You will then be able to create your project once the hackathon starts. To be eligible for the judging process you'll have to submit the project before the hackathon ends.
Do we need to have the entire idea working?
The entire idea need to be fully or partially implemented based on the discretion of judges after reviewing the idea and implementation.
I don't want to hack, can I still get involved?
If you're looking to be a volunteer or mentor (i.e. someone with technical skills who'd be willing to help out participants during the weekend), you should reach out to us at hackthehourglass@gmail.com.
Can I start working on my hack before the event?
No, in the interest of fairness, students should not be working on their projects before the hackathon begins and we will not allow participants to work on pre-existing projects. However, you can familiarize yourself with all the tools and technologies you intend to use beforehand!
